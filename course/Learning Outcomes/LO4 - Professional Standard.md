You take responsibility when solving ICT issues. You define and carry out your applied research using relevant selected methodologies and provide advice to your stakeholders in complex and uncertain contexts. You substantiate and validate future-oriented choices by use of law, ethical, intercultural, and sustainable arguments. 

## Further clarification 

### Take responsibility  

- You identify, coordinate and optimise subtasks within your project.  
- You are transparent about the impact of the solutions you create.  
- You ensure the transfer of your work is efficient for the stakeholder. 
    
### Applied research

- You formulate appropriate subjects or questions to investigate within the context and can prioritise them. 
- You justify your choices of research patterns, strategies and methods used. 
- You demonstrate the reliability, validity and relevance of your research. 
- You reflect critically on the followed research process. 

### Complex and uncertain contexts

- You learn the domain of the project, speak the language of the domain to communicate with stakeholders. 
- You differentiate between essential and accidental complexity and advise the stakeholder to ameliorate the accidental complexity.  

### Law, ethical, intercultural and sustainable: 

- You recognise and respect ethical boundaries — your own and those of others — and act with integrity and accountability. 
- You apply ethical standards and norms in the field of expertise, e.g. the GDPR or the AI Act. 
- You independently perform an impact and stakeholder analysis and provide your client with sound ethical and sustainable advice. 
- You collaborate in diverse and intercultural teams. You effectively manage cultural differences in your interactions with stakeholders.
